Projects

Independent Maker Projects

After participating in the High School MakerSpace for several years, an independent research and development route was taken in senior year, using the knowledge learned from said MakerSpace to develop several projects:

DUX

The Dangerously Useful Xenagogue

A small rover with an LCD that was controlled through either a website or a mobile application. Used the ESP8266 to relay information to/from the website and the Arduino to control the rover.

MAN

The Meteorologist Assistant Novelty

An RC car made from scratch through either a small controller or hand gestures (using gyroscopes) whose primary purpose was towing behind the following project...

WISE

The Weather Information Sensor Extraordinaire

A small station on wheels that could relay information such as humidity (air and soil), CO2 levels and temperature to the IBM Watson IOT platform to be recorded and then displayed periodically in graphs.

A lot of these were mainly prototypes, attempting to create robotic crafts geared towards interaction with humans, all early attempts at creating “companions” for personal or professional environments.
